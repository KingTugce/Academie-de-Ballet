Our Philosophy

Ballet is not just
a discipline. It is a
way of seeing the world.

Founded on the belief that rigorous classical training and genuine artistic expression are inseparable, Académie de Ballet offers a curriculum grounded in the Vaganova and Royal Academy traditions — taught with warmth, clarity, and deep respect for the art form.

We welcome students of every background who come with curiosity, commitment, and a love of movement. Whether your child takes their first barre class at age three or you return to dance as an adult, this is a place where you will be seen, challenged, and celebrated.

What age can my child start ballet?
+

Our Early Childhood program welcomes students from age three. At this stage, classes focus on music, movement, and imagination rather than formal technique — building the physical and emotional foundation for later study. Children typically begin structured ballet training around age six in our Children's Ballet division.

Do you offer a trial class before committing?
+

Yes. We warmly invite prospective students and families to attend a complimentary trial class in the appropriate division. This gives your child the opportunity to experience our studio environment and meet their teacher before any commitment is made.

What do students need to wear to class?
+

Students in the Children's and Student Divisions wear a uniform consistent with their level — typically a specific leotard colour, pink tights, and leather ballet slippers. Details are provided upon enrolment. Adult open class students may wear any comfortable ballet attire and soft ballet shoes.

How does the Pre-Professional program work?
+

The Pre-Professional Division is our most rigorous program, designed for serious students who aspire to a career in dance. Entry is by audition. Students train multiple days per week in classical technique, pointe, pas de deux, contemporary work, and repertoire. Faculty maintain relationships with company directors and summer program coordinators to support each student's individual career path.

Is prior experience required for adult classes?
+

No prior experience is necessary. Our Adult Open Classes welcome complete beginners, those returning after a long break, and experienced dancers. Multiple levels are offered throughout the week, and our faculty are skilled at guiding a varied range of experience within a single class with grace and clarity.

What is your tuition structure?
+

Tuition varies by program level and number of classes per week. We offer monthly billing, semester packages, and limited merit-based scholarships for students with demonstrated financial need and artistic promise. A full schedule and tuition guide is available upon request — please contact us to receive it.
